Polish Digraphs

Combination Pronunciation Hint Notes
Ch like English h Phonetically identical to h
Cz like ch in chocolate Harder than ć
Dz like ds in kids Only before vowels
Dź soft j, like jeans Softer than dz
Dż like j in jungle Hard version of dź
Rz like ż (hard zh) Different spelling contexts
Sz like sh in shut Harder than ś

Important distinctions: Ś vs Sz, Ć vs Cz, Ź vs Ż, Dź vs Dż — the first is softer, the second harder.

The 7 Polish Grammar Cases

Key questions, usage, and details with examples

1. Mianownik (Nominative)

Kto? Co?

Subject of the sentence.

2. Dopełniacz (Genitive)

Kogo? Czego?

Possession, absence, quantity.

3. Celownik (Dative)

Komu? Czemu?

Indirect object (to/for someone).

4. Biernik (Accusative)

Kogo? Co?

Direct object (who/what is affected).

5. Narzędnik (Instrumental)

Z kim? Z czym?

With/by – tools, companions, professions.

6. Miejscownik (Locative)

O kim? O czym?

Only after prepositions (in, on, about, at).

7. Wołacz (Vocative)

—

When addressing or calling someone directly.
